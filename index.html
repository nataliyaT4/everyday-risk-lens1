What is being measured?

Worry = % saying they are very or somewhat worried the risk could cause serious harm.

Experienced harm = % saying they personally experienced serious harm in the past two years.

These are self-reported global averages, not objective exposure or official injury statistics.

5. Source reliability lens

Use this section to make a balanced judgement. A source can be useful and reliable for one purpose, but limited for another.

Why this source is useful Why you should use it with caution
Large global survey: more than 143,000 interviews across 140 countries and territories. Large-scale survey data still depends on sampling, question wording, translation and fieldwork conditions.
Produced by Lloyd’s Register Foundation with Gallup fieldwork and analysis. The report has an institutional purpose: improving safety. That does not make it unreliable, but it may shape the framing.
Directly measures people’s reported worry and experience of harm. Self-reported data is not the same as objective exposure, official injuries or verified medical/environmental records.
The report distinguishes between worry, harm and policy implications. Global averages can hide national, regional and social inequalities.
Suggested reliability sentence frame

“I would treat this report as a useful and fairly reliable source for comparing reported perceptions and experiences of everyday risk globally, because … However, I would not use it alone to prove objective levels of danger, because …”
